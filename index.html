Discover Your Perfect Workout

Whether you're a seasoned athlete or just starting your fitness journey, there's a class out there that fits your body, your goals, and your vibe. Explore our guide to today's most popular studio fitness classes — from high-intensity sweat sessions to mindful movement practices — and find the one that moves you.

Barre

A ballet-inspired workout that blends small, isometric movements with elements of Pilates, yoga, and strength training. Don't let the graceful name fool you — your muscles will be shaking in minutes.

  • Intensity: Low to Moderate
  • Duration: 45–60 minutes
  • What to Bring: Grip socks (required at most studios), water bottle
  • Best For: Toning, improving posture, building core strength, low-impact conditioning
  • Workout Type: Strength

HIIT

Short bursts of all-out effort followed by brief rest periods — HIIT is designed to push your limits and maximize calorie burn in minimal time. Every class is different, but expect to sweat.

  • Intensity: High
  • Duration: 30–45 minutes
  • What to Bring: Sneakers, towel, water bottle
  • Best For: Weight loss, cardiovascular fitness, building endurance
  • Workout Type: Cardio

Lagree

Lagree is often mistaken for Pilates, but it's a beast of its own. Performed on a machine called the Megaformer, Lagree uses slow, controlled movements under constant tension to simultaneously build strength, endurance, and flexibility — all while keeping your heart rate elevated.

  • Intensity: Moderate to High
  • Duration: 45–50 minutes
  • What to Bring: Grip socks (required), water bottle
  • Best For: Full-body toning, core strength, low-impact but high-results training
  • Workout Type: Strength

Reformer Pilates

Performed on a spring-resistance machine called the Reformer, this class focuses on controlled, precise movements that target deep stabilizing muscles. It's low-impact but surprisingly challenging, and leaves you feeling longer and stronger.

  • Intensity: Low to Moderate
  • Duration: 50–60 minutes
  • What to Bring: Grip socks (required at most studios), comfortable fitted clothing
  • Best For: Rehabilitation, core strength, flexibility, body awareness
  • Workout Type: Mind-Body

Spin Class

A high-energy indoor cycling class set to pumping music, with an instructor guiding you through climbs, sprints, and intervals. It's a serious cardio workout that's easy on the joints — and incredibly addictive.

  • Intensity: Moderate to High
  • Duration: 45–60 minutes
  • What to Bring: Cycling shoes (or ask about rentals), padded shorts optional, towel, water bottle
  • Best For: Cardiovascular endurance, leg strength, stress relief, calorie burn
  • Workout Type: Cardio

Yoga

A mind-body practice rooted in breathwork, flexibility, and intentional movement. Classes vary widely — from a traditional flow to a heated session (practiced in a room warmed to 95–105°F) to a Yoga-Pilates fusion that blends flowing yoga sequences with mat Pilates core work for a more strength-focused practice.

  • Intensity: Low to Moderate (varies by style)
  • Duration: 60–75 minutes
  • What to Bring: Yoga mat, towel (especially for heated), water bottle
  • Best For: Stress relief, flexibility, mindfulness, active recovery
  • Workout Type: Mind-Body

Mat Pilates

No machine needed — just your mat and your core. Mat Pilates focuses on controlled movements that strengthen the deep muscles of your abdomen, back, and hips. It's accessible, equipment-free, and deceptively hard.

  • Intensity: Low to Moderate
  • Duration: 45–60 minutes
  • What to Bring: Yoga mat, comfortable fitted clothing, water bottle
  • Best For: Core strength, posture, flexibility, injury prevention
  • Workout Type: Mind-Body
